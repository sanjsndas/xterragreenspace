🌱 The Story Behind Xterra Green Space

Founded in 2015 by lifelong horticulturist Elena Marigold, Xterra Green Space began as a tiny urban rooftop garden in the heart of MeadowVista. What started as an experiment in yellow-themed companion planting quickly blossomed into a movement. Elena, a master gardener with a passion for heirloom varieties, noticed that golden petals attracted not only bees but also a sense of wonder and community. Neighbors gathered to watch sunflowers turn their heads, children tasted 'Yellow Pear' tomatoes for the first time, and soon the "Golden Block Club" was born.

Over the years, Xterra Green Space expanded to a 12-acre demonstration farm, education center, and seed library. We've hosted over 350+ workshops, from permaculture design to therapeutic horticulture. Our signature 'Yellow Therapy Garden' has become a blueprint for assisted living facilities across the state. Today, we remain a grassroots organization rooted in regenerative agriculture and joyful learning. Every class, every plant sale, and every digital guide carries the same mission: to help you grow abundance with the sunniest palette on earth.

In 2022, we launched our global online gardening hub, making our research and yellow-themed resources accessible to millions. Whether you have a windowsill or an acre, Xterra Green Space walks with you — spade in hand, marigold in heart.

💛 Our Philosophy: The Golden Thread of Regeneration

At Xterra Green Space, we don't just garden — we cultivate relationships. The "yellow thread" symbolizes the interconnection between soil health, plant vibrancy, pollinator abundance, and human well-being. Every decision, from selecting open-pollinated seeds to hosting free seed swaps, is guided by ecological integrity. We are a plastic-free campus, using biodegradable pots, compostable labels, and rainwater-fed irrigation. Our nursery grows over 150 species of yellow-flowering natives, including threatened species like the golden monkeyflower. We practice 'carbon farming': our hedgerows and silvopasture areas sequester an estimated 24 tons of CO₂ annually. But beyond numbers, we invite you to experience the pure delight of harvesting a sun-warmed yellow squash or watching a goldfinch feast on rudbeckia seeds. That joy is why we exist.

We also advocate for food justice: through our 'Golden Harvest Share', we donate fresh produce to local food banks (over 7,200 lbs in 2024 alone). Our sliding-scale workshops ensure that low-income families have access to seeds, tools, and training. Xterra Green Space believes that gardening should be a right, not a privilege. The yellow color reminds us to be beacons of hope — resilient, radiant, and ever-growing. Join us in this movement, one seed at a time.
